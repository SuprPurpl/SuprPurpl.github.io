<ol>: Used to make ordered lists
<ul>: Used to make unordered lists
<li>: Entries in lists will be flanked by this tag
<dl>: Used to make a definition list
<dt>: Contains the term being defined
<dd>: Contains the definition of the term

  1. Keep muzzle aimed at the ground/down-range and observe correct Trigger discipline throughout the procedure
  2. Check the breach for any unfired rounds (Remove if found)
  3. Insert magazine
  4. Firmly pull back on the slide, placing a round in the chamber
  5. Confirm that the saftey is disengaged
The weapon is now loaded and ready to fire.

Images

Top
Firstly, before we learn about images...

Yeah, yeah, the pig's cute, but back to business!
The line responsible:
<img src="img/CutePig1.jpg" alt-"A cute little piggy" width="300" height="300" title="This lil' piggy is cute!" />

Ok, I'm gonna break down the above element:

  1. src=: this is the image's source, hence why its followed by "img/CutePig1.jpg"
  2. alt=: this provides text/description for the picture if you can't see it.
  3. The height and width obviously govern the size of the image...like, duh
  4. title=: this makes "This piggy lil' is cute" appear when you hover the cursor over the image.

Image Placement

I'm not gonna make loads of examples, but bascially,
the picture will show up in different places depending
on where you place the element.

  1. If you want the image above the text:
    2. <img src="img/CutePig1.jpg" alt="A cute little piggy" width="300" height="300" title="This lil' piggy is cute!" />
    <p>
  2. If you want the text to start alongside the iamge:
    3. <p>
    <img src="img/CutePig1.jpg" alt="A cute little piggy" width="300" height="300" title="This lil' piggy is cute!" />
  3. If you want the image mid-way through the paragraph/text:
    4. Blah, Blah, Blah, Pigs, Blah <p>
    <img src="img/CutePig1.jpg" alt="A cute little piggy" width="300" height="300" title="This lil' piggy is cute!" /> Blah, Blah, Blah, Pigs, Blah
Get it? Good.
You can also choose what side of the text the image is on using the align= attribute.
Just add (align="right") or (align="left")it into the element...
AND you can align the first line of text with the Top, Middle or Bottom of the image,
by using (align="top") or (align="middle") or (align="bottom")...
Still easy? Good.
Remember to save images in the correct format and you should be fine.

Tables

Top

Ok, tables, you know what tables do...
They're just big bunches of info that have been arranged nicely.

Tags you're gonna be using:
<table>: Signifies the start of a table.
<tr>: Indicates the start of each row.
<td>: Stands for (table data) it's what goes in the boxes.
<th>: Table heading (Names for rows and column contents).
<thead>: Makes table headings.
<tbody>: Governs what's within the main body of the table.
<tfoot>: Final results and foot notes go in the footer.

Here's what a table looks like:

Hand-Cannon Scout-Rifle Auto-Rifle Pulse-Rifle
Impact: High Mid Mid Low
Range: Close Mid-Long Close-Mid Close-Mid
RoF: Slow Medium Fast Medium
Mag Size: 6-13 rounds 10-24 rounds 20-50+ rounds 17-32 round
Reload Speed: Slow/Variable Medium Medium Fast
I also used the attributes cellpadding, cellspacing, border, bgcolor.
Dont forget colspan and rowspan for obvious reasons.
